1854: The Soho Cholera Outbreak

A deadly cholera epidemic struck Soho in summer 1854. Dr. John Snow and others mapped cases to investigate how cholera spread.

Houses: Deaths vs. Non-Deaths

Each house is mapped: red circles show cholera deaths; gray show no deaths.

Sewer Grates & Ventilators

Victorian theory blamed “miasma” from sewer vents. Here are all sewer grates & ventilators.

The Pest Field Hypothesis

Some blamed gasses from a 17th-century pest field. Highlight houses on the old pest field.

Water Pumps

John Snow suspected cholera was waterborne. These are the eight water pumps—especially the Broad Street pump in pink.

Distance-Decay Rings

Cholera deaths concentrate near the Broad Street pump. These 5 m rings show distance-decay.

Conclusion

Snow removed the pump handle, ending the outbreak. His work laid the foundation for modern epidemiology.
